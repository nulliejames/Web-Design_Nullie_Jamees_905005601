BRIEF HISTORY

The Sierra Leone Football Association (SLFA) was established in 1960, the same year Sierra Leone gained independence. For over six decades, we have been at the forefront of football development in our nation, navigating challenges and celebrating triumphs. From our early days organizing local competitions to our current role as a member of FIFA, CAF, and WAFU, SLFA has consistently worked to elevate Sierra Leonean football on both the national and international stages.

1960
SLFA founded as the governing body for football in newly independent Sierra Leone.
1967
Sierra Leone joins FIFA, gaining international recognition and support.
1974
First international victory against Nigeria in the African Nations Cup qualifiers.
1994
Leone Stars qualify for African Cup of Nations for the first time, reaching quarter-finals.
2010
Launch of the Sierra Leone Women's Premier League, promoting gender equality in sports.
2023
Completion of nationwide youth development centers across all regions.

OUR VISION

To establish Sierra Leone as a formidable football nation in Africa, recognized for producing world-class talent, maintaining professional standards, and using football as a catalyst for social development, national unity, and youth empowerment. We envision a future where every child in Sierra Leone has access to quality football education and opportunities to excel both on and off the pitch.

OUR MISSION

To develop, promote, and organize football at all levels throughout Sierra Leone by implementing sustainable programs, building quality infrastructure, nurturing talent from grassroots to elite levels, and fostering international partnerships. We are committed to transparency, good governance, and creating pathways for Sierra Leonean footballers to achieve both national pride and international success.

OUR ACHIEVEMENTS

Over the years, SLFA has achieved significant milestones that have shaped football in Sierra Leone:

  • Qualified for the Africa Cup of Nations three times (1994, 1996, 2022)
  • Developed over 50 professional players who have played in international leagues
  • Established 16 regional football academies across Sierra Leone
  • Introduced the Women's Premier League with 10 competitive teams
  • Hosted successful international tournaments including WAFU Zone A competitions
  • Implemented nationwide grassroots programs reaching over 10,000 youth annually
  • Upgraded 5 major stadiums to meet international standards
